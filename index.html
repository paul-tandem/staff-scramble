FAQ

What is the point of this game?

Staff Scramble is a lighthearted look at a serious risk: Key Person Risk. In the game, your "character" represents a vital employee. The hazards they face—flu season, sudden resignations, or even a simple vacation—are real-world events that can halt your organization if your processes aren't institutionalized.

What is "Key Person Risk"?

Key Person Risk (KPR) happens when an organization relies too heavily on individual people for specific knowledge or skills. If that person "scrambles" (leaves, takes ill, or is promoted), the organization is in jeopardy. Our goal is to help you move from an organization depending on a few key people to a process-driven one.

What is the "Bus Factor"?

It represents the risk that comes from knowledge and abilities not being shared among team members. If a key person were to be "hit by a bus" (or suddenly become unavailable), the lack of shared expertise would cause an organization to stall or fail.

Why are these "hazards" a problem?

In a perfect world, your organization runs like a machine. In the real world, people get sick, burnt out, or move on to new opportunities. If your SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures) and automations live only in an employee's head, your organization is effectively "scrambling" every single day.

How do I stop the scramble?

By building systems that outlast any individual contributor. This involves:

  • Documenting your core organization processes.
  • Automating repetitive tasks to reduce manual errors.
  • Cross-training team members so there is no "single point of failure."
